SAFER Program

Student Aid for Field Epidemiology Response

The SAFER program provides hands-on experience with state and county health departments during real disease outbreaks. Students conduct disease surveillance at major events including the Super Bowl, MLB All-Star Game, and Fiesta Bowl, gaining practical skills that translate directly to careers at CDC, state health departments, and beyond.

One Health Initiative

PAWS: Prescribing Antibiotics Wisely in Animal Shelters

A collaborative pilot study with the College of Veterinary Medicine addressing antimicrobial resistance in animal shelters. Using mixed-methods research combining key informant interviews with shelter professionals and retrospective reviews of health records, we're developing practical stewardship guidance that minimizes AMR spread among animals, humans, and the environment.

Funding: One Health Pilot Grant, University of Arizona (2024-2025)
Team: Ellingson (PI), Wishnie (Co-PI), Paulina Colombo, Timothy Ojodare

DARPA Award Winner

Spatial Temporal Epidemic Prediction

Our model of epidemic growth won first prize in the DARPA/InnoCentive Forecasting Chikungunya Challenge. We blend field collections, ecological assessment, laboratory experiments, epidemiological analysis, spatial statistics, remote sensing, GIS, and computer modeling. Research includes work at Biosphere-2 on vector flight patterns and collaboration with ADHS on climate adaptation projects.

Current research: West Nile virus, dengue, canine heartworm, Valley fever, H. pylori and gastric cancer
Dr. Heidi Brown awarded Fulbright to study climate effects on mosquito-borne disease transmission
